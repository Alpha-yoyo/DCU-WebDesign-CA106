Most Popular Chinese Festivals

Spring Festival

Spring Festival (Chinese New Year)

Chinese Spring Festival is probably the grandest and most important festival in China. The festival begins on the first day of the first month of the traditional Chinese calendar and ends with Lantern Festival which is on the 15th day of the same month. Similar to the Christmas holiday, it is the time where the whole family gathered around, enjoy delicious foods and happy times.


Yuanxiao Festival

Yuanxiao Festival

The tradition Lantern Festival or Yuan Xiao Festival marks the end of the Chinese Spring Festival. It falls on the 15th day of the first lunar month. The main activities on the day would be to eat Yuanxiao, small balls made of glutinous rice flour with different kinds of fillings.


Mid-Autumn Festival

Mid-Autumn Festival

Mid-Autumn Festival is a traditional Chinese that originates from the worship of the moon. It falls on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month and symbolizes harvest and family reunion. Traditionally, family members will gather around, enjoy the full moon while eating delicious mooncakes.


Duanwu Festival

Duanwu Festival

The Duanwu Festival also known as the Dragon Boat Festivals, falls on the fifth day of the fifth Chinese lunar month. People would bring their dragon-boats out and celebrate the day through the dragon-boat race, typically in China's southern part of areas, another important thing is to eat Zong Zi which is glutinous rice with fillings wrapped around with reed leaves.


Chongyang Festival

Chongyang Festival

The Chongyang Festival, also known as Double Ninth Festival, is celebrated on the ninth day of the ninth month of the Chinese lunar calendar. During this time, people will eat Chongyang cake and drink chrysanthemum wine, others would prefer to climb the mountains or admire beautiful chrysanthemums.
